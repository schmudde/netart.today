do you know Light on the Net, 1996 by Masaki Fujihata?

Yes, "Light on the Net" is an interactive art installation created by Masaki Fujihata in 1996. It is considered one of the pioneering works in the field of internet-based art. This piece utilized the early internet to create a real-time, interactive experience for users.

Background and Concept

"Light on the Net" was an innovative project at its time, reflecting the growing possibilities of digital and networked art. Fujihata used the internet to connect physical spaces with digital interactions, emphasizing the interplay between virtual and physical realms.

How It Works

The installation involved a physical space equipped with a grid of light bulbs, which could be controlled via the internet. Users from around the world could access a web interface to interact with the installation, turning individual light bulbs on and off. This interaction created a dynamic light display in the physical installation space, reflecting the collective input of remote participants.

Significance

  • Interactivity: The piece emphasized user participation, making it one of the early examples of interactive art that leveraged the internet.
  • Connectivity: It highlighted the emerging possibilities of global connectivity, showing how digital networks could influence and change physical environments.
  • Innovation: Fujihata's work is often noted for its technical and conceptual innovation, anticipating many themes and techniques that would become central to digital and internet art.

Legacy

"Light on the Net" is an important milestone in the history of digital art. It paved the way for future explorations of networked interactivity and the blending of digital and physical spaces. The work is often discussed in the context of the evolution of internet art and interactive installations.

import imageio
import numpy as np

# Load the base image
image_path = '/mnt/data/A_hall_at_Gifu_Softopia_Center_at_midnight_on_31st.png'
image = imageio.imread(image_path)

# Function to modify the lights (turn on/off)
def modify_lights(image, intensity):
    # Create a mask to identify light bulbs (simple thresholding)
    mask = image[:, :, 0] > 200  # Assuming lights are bright in the red channel
    
    # Copy image to modify
    modified_image = image.copy()
    
    # Apply intensity to the lights
    for channel in range(3):  # Apply to R, G, B channels
        modified_image[:, :, channel][mask] = modified_image[:, :, channel][mask] * intensity
    
    return modified_image

# Generate frames for the GIF
frames = []
for i in np.linspace(0.2, 1.0, 10):  # Dimming and brightening the lights
    modified_image = modify_lights(image, i)
    frames.append(modified_image)

# Save the frames as an animated GIF
output_gif_path = '/mnt/data/light_on_the_net_animation.gif'
imageio.mimsave(output_gif_path, frames, duration=0.3)

on_mask = image[:, :, 0] > 200  # Lights that will turn on
